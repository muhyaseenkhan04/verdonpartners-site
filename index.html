About Averdon Partners

Executive & technical search for regulated, safety-critical environments

Averdon Partners is built for organisations where a senior hire is a decision about risk, resilience and reputation – not just a vacancy to be filled.

We focus on aviation, aerospace and complex engineering businesses where:

  • Downtime and disruption have a direct commercial and customer impact
  • Regulators, lessors, owners and insurers are paying attention
  • Technical decisions flow through to operations, contracts and brand

Typical sponsors include COOs, CEOs, Accountable Managers, Heads of Maintenance and Heads of Engineering who need a search partner able to talk fluently across operations, engineering, finance and safety.

On every mandate we:

  • Treat the brief as a commercial and risk case, not a shopping list of skills
  • Map the relevant market in detail before committing to a shortlist
  • Test technical depth, behavioural fit and operating context – not just titles and logos
  • Only introduce people you can put in front of a regulator, board or owner with confidence

How we work

Low-volume, research-led, founder-driven search

Our process is built for regulated, asset-heavy environments where the cost of a bad hire is measured in safety, availability and reputation as well as recruitment fees. We behave more like a specialist advisory firm than a high-volume agency: disciplined, documented and with clear, low-noise communication throughout.

The firm is led by an aerospace engineering graduate with hands-on maintenance exposure and a strong grounding in contracts, liability and governance. That mix of technical literacy and legal awareness shapes how we take a brief, challenge it and represent you in the market.

01. Define
Brief & mandate definition

We start with your operating reality: fleet and asset profile, regulatory exposure, stakeholder expectations and internal politics. The brief is shaped around risk and outcomes, not just a job description.

02. Map
Research & market mapping

We build a structured view of the market: where the right people sit, how they are measured, and what would move them. Target lists and approach strategy are agreed before outreach begins.

03. Assess
Shortlist & structured interviews

Candidates are assessed against technical depth, leadership behaviour and fit with your operating environment. You see a small, defensible shortlist – not a stack of loosely related CVs.

04. Close
Offer, onboarding & follow-up

We support offer design, resignation management and onboarding. After placement, we stay close to both sides to ensure the hire lands as intended and the first 6–12 months track to plan.

Example searches

Illustrative executive & technical search briefs

Live assignments are confidential. The anonymised examples below show the level, type and context of the mandates we typically support across aviation, aerospace and asset-heavy operations.

Airline · Line Maintenance
Head of Line Maintenance – European Airline

Fleet of narrow-body aircraft operating high-frequency routes. Search focused on improving on-time performance, stabilising rostered coverage and tightening safety and compliance reporting across multiple bases.

MRO · Base Maintenance
Base Maintenance Manager – Independent MRO

Multi-bay facility with mixed fleet inputs. Mandate centred on leadership of hangar productivity, turnaround times and coordination with planning, materials, commercial and quality teams.

OEM · Engineering
Engineering Manager – Aerospace OEM

Functional lead across systems and structures teams on a long-cycle programme. Focus on design assurance, certification interface and integration with manufacturing engineering and customer support.

Infrastructure · Operations
Head of Operations – Asset-heavy Infrastructure

Leadership role in a regulated infrastructure business, responsible for asset availability, maintenance strategy and control-room operations in a multi-stakeholder, 24/7 environment.

Group · Technical Leadership
Group Technical Director – Aviation Group

Group-level mandate overseeing technical standards, fleet strategy and airworthiness governance across multiple AOCs, with direct interaction with regulators, lessors and investor stakeholders.

Future-facing
COO / Operations Director – Industrial Technology

Future-facing brief within an industrial technology or infrastructure-backed platform, bringing operations, engineering and asset management under a single accountable leader to support growth and investment.
